# Recommendation Memo

## Business Problem Summary

The company launched a new onboarding and activation campaign to improve user conversion and early engagement.

Users were divided into two groups:

- Control Group: Existing onboarding experience
- Treatment Group: New campaign experience

Leadership needs to decide whether the new campaign should be launched for all users.

The decision will impact future users who go through the onboarding process.

## Objective

The main objective is to determine whether the new onboarding campaign improves business performance compared to the existing experience.

The primary success metric for this experiment is Paid Conversion Rate because converting users into paying customers directly contributes to subscription revenue and business growth.

## Risks to Monitor

The decision should not be based only on conversion improvement. The company must also monitor:

- Refund rate
- Support ticket rate
- Engagement quality
- Days required to convert
- Segment-level performance differences

## Decision Approach

The final recommendation will be based on:

- Comparison between Control and Treatment groups
- Experiment KPI analysis
- Statistical hypothesis testing
- Guardrail metric evaluation

The campaign will be recommended for launch only if it improves the primary metric without creating significant negative impacts.
